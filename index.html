Get on the Water

A boat launch in The Hague should be:

Fitting

Small sailboats, not motorboats. Kayaks, not jet skis. Walk-in launches, not towed-in by a car. The Ghent section of Norfolk is a historic masterpiece. A boat launch will amplify, not distract from, this area's unique setting on the historic Elizabeth River.

Convenient

The Hague Boat Launch seeks to make waterway access the most convenient to Norfolk residents through providing easy access to launching their watercraft.

Accessible

Through up front design, the boat launch will deliberately plan the watercraft types to be supported. A launch will need to support Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) standards, kayaks, and small sailboats.

Ramp Design

There are hundreds of possible ramp designs. The National Park Service defines many in their document - Logical Lasting Launches

Floating Launch

Typically composed of a deck, frame, and floats, they are anchored to the shore. Paddlers launch from the deck, which is supported by the frame, while the floats beneath the frame provide buoyancy

Would require pilings to be sufficiently high to anchor float in flood tides

Typically require 3 feet of water at all times

Adjusts to fluctuating water levels common in Smith's Creek

Potential concern about impact of freezing

Fixed Ramp

Concrete ramps may be used as launches by themselves or in combination with floating launches, piers, bridges, dock abutments, bulkheads, and rock cribs

Would require method to increase traction (e.g. V-grooves)

Durable; not subject to rot or rust; not subject to tidal flooding problems

Question of aesthetics - although mitigated with finishing methods with river rocks, fieldstones, or salt-finishing

Concern over cleaning if there is mud build-up from flooding

Surface can be slippery

Modular Floats

Polyethylene, the most frequently chosen plastic, can function as both frame and float; they may have grooves and brackets that attach easily to shoreline structures or floats may have built-in cleats, which facilitate anchoring

Cheaper than other floating methods

Concern over aesthetics

Same concerns about freezing, requiring 3 feet of depth

Same benefit with adjusting for flood tides
