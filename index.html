Get on the Water

A boat launch in The Hague should be:

Fitting

Small sailboats, not motorboats. Kayaks, not jet skis. Walk-in launches, not towed-in by a car. The Ghent section of Norfolk is a historical masterpiece. A boat launch will amplify, not distract from, this area's unique setting on the historical Elizabeth River.

Convenient

The Hague Boat Launch seeks to make waterway access the most convenient to Norfolk residents through providing easy access to launching their watercraft.

Accessible

Through up front design, the boat launch will deliberately plan the watercraft types to be supported. A launch will need to support Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) standards, kayaks, and small sailboats.

Dundaff and Fairfax

On the west side of Smith's Creek, this potential location is nearby the Stone Park and Stockley Gardens.

Pros

  • Large amount of unused, city-owned land
  • Substantial parking available
  • Walking distance to many residents

Cons

  • Potential inconvenience to residents on Dundaff
  • Need to check depth

Mowbray and Botetourt

On the East side of Smith's Creek, this potential location is nearby the Hague footbridge and the Chrysler Museum.

Pros

  • Nearby Chrysler Museum
  • Substantial parking available

Cons

  • Small sailboats prevented due to footbridge height
  • Existing launch (Stairs leading into water)

Mowbray and Fairfax

Pros

  • Walking distance to many residents

Cons

  • Potential inconvenience to residents in The Hague
  • Limited parking

Boat Types

The following boat types are intended for support:

  • Kayaks
  • Canoes
  • Small sailboats (e.g. Laser, Sunfish, etc)
  • Stand Up Paddleboard

The following boat types are specifically excluded from support:

  • Larger Boats
  • Jet Skis
  • Any boats being trailered
  • Other
